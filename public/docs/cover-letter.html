Cover letter to Lewi Stone (template)

A short, calibrated email that does the introduction, states the ask, and gets out of the way. Edit the angle-bracketed bits.

How to use: the email below is calibrated for a working academic. Three deliberate moves: (1) open with a specific reference to their work, not yours; (2) state in one sentence what you built and the empirical headline; (3) ask for one concrete thing. Length matters — under 200 words. Anything longer signals you don't respect their time.

Notes on each move

  1. Subject line: Specific, no buzzwords. The phrase "structured-output prompting framework" tells him what kind of object this is in the first 3 words. The phrase "N=90 across model scales" signals you have data, not vibes. Cap at ~80 chars.
  2. First paragraph: Open by referencing his work. Pick one specific paper or method he is known for. (For Lewi Stone: his COVID-Israel modelling paper, his conservation-network resilience papers, or his Lotka-Volterra–style applied work — pick the closest one to your tool's framing.) This proves you have done at least one hour of homework.
  3. Second paragraph: State the artefact + the empirical headline + the falsifiability hook. Do not oversell. The numbers should speak for themselves — and they do: 100% adherence at three scales is a real finding, but it is a small one, and you should signal that you know it is small.
  4. Two reasons for him specifically: One methodological (his expertise touches yours), one social (politely asking permission to forward to Jacob — Lewi is the gatekeeper, this acknowledges that without grovelling).
  5. The ask: One concrete, time-bounded request. 30 minutes. Within two weeks. Zoom or in person. And a graceful exit ("a one-line yes/no is useful") so he can decline without feeling rude. Both are respectful.
  6. Length: 180 words in the body. Anything longer signals nervousness; anything shorter signals carelessness. This length is calibrated.
  7. Tone: No exclamation marks. No "I would love" or "I'd be thrilled". Calm, substantive, calibrated. Match his register.

What to attach

  • Single PDF: the Academic Brief (/docs/academic-brief.html → ⌘P → Save as PDF). One page. The numbers will be live by the time you send.
  • One link in the email body: to the Stats Report (/docs/stats.html) so he can drill into raw data if he wants.
  • Do not attach the pitch deck, the executive summary, or the README. Those are for a different audience and clutter the academic ask.

If he says yes

  1. Confirm time + Zoom link / RMIT room within 24 hours.
  2. Send a one-line agenda the day before: "15 min: I demo the tool on a question of yours. 10 min: you push on the calibration claim. 5 min: I ask if it would be appropriate to share with Jacob."
  3. Show up 5 min early. Have the tool already running on your laptop with the academic voice toggled on. Have one of his recent papers' open-question section already in the textarea.
  4. Do not type during the call. Listen.

If he says no

Reply with one line of thanks, no follow-up, no defensiveness. Then send the same email — adapted — directly to Jacob, opening with "Lewi Stone suggested I might write to you given your work on …" only if Lewi gave you that permission. Otherwise write to Jacob fresh, no name-drop.
