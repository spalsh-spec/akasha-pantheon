Raw benchmark evidence. Every chart traces back to data/benchmark.json. Re-execute via node scripts/benchmark.mjs.

Method

Five questions selected for breadth (mathematical, neurochemical, psychological, comparative-religious, mechanical) were sent through both oracle providers via the same /api/oracle endpoint. Each response was parsed for the six required keys (LITERAL · MYTHIC · FUZZY · SIGIL · TWEET · CONFIDENCE) and scored on adherence, tradition citation, latency, character length, and self-reported confidence.

N = 10

5 questions × 2 providers (Ollama gemma3:4b on M1; Cloudflare Workers AI Llama 3.3 70B FP8 Fast). All runs completed without error. Raw response payloads captured in benchmark.json.

The five questions

  1. Why does π appear in probability?
  2. What is the physics of flow state?
  3. What did Jung mean by the collective unconscious — is it measurable?
  4. Why do all major religions have a flood myth?
  5. What is leverage, in physics and in life?

5 · Findings

  1. Format adherence is reliable across model scales. Both a 4B and a 70B model produced 6/6 keys on every run. The structural prior is robust.
  2. Latency is acceptable for both modes. FAST stays under 21s on the worst case; DEEP stays under 7s. Both well under the 30s threshold for reflective use.
  3. The smaller model overshoots on confidence. Mean confidence 0.89 for FAST vs 0.82 for DEEP. A larger model is more willing to say "I don't know with full certainty." This is a feature.
  4. Tradition naming is honoured. Mean traditions cited per answer is 1.6 (FAST) and 1.2 (DEEP). Vague "ancient wisdom" responses did not occur.
  5. Compactness emerges from the larger model. DEEP averages 745 chars per answer vs FAST's 984. The 70B is more concise and quote-able.
