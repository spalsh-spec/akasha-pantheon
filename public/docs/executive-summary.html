⟁AKASHA · PANTHEON · executive summary
10 May 2026 · v0.2.0

A fuzzy-truth oracle that holds three truths at once.

Local-first, free, calibrated, and structurally pluralist by design.

The problem. The current generation of AI assistants is trained to give one answer. When the answer is empirical, that's right. When the question lives between empirical, mythic, and synthetic registers — most questions a real person actually asks — collapsing it to one answer is dishonest. The model either picks an authority and pretends, or hedges into uselessness.

The system. Akasha Pantheon is a structured-output interface to large language models. It forces every answer into five parallel slots:

  • Literal — what consensus science / mainstream history says, with a citation if available.
  • Mythic — how a specific named tradition (Vedic, Hermetic, Taoist, Yoruba, Hopi…) framed it.
  • Fuzzy — what synthesis is available when both are taken seriously.
  • Sigil — one symbolic phrase, 3–7 words.
  • Tweet — a 240-character compression you can stand behind.

Plus a self-reported confidence from 0.00 to 1.00, present on every answer. The 5-section format is enforced by the system prompt and verified at parse time; non-conformant outputs are not displayed as answers.

Why this matters

This is the first interface that operationalizes epistemic pluralism — the recognition that there are multiple legitimate registers of knowledge — without descending into relativism. The Literal slot still cites a constant, a date, a law. The Mythic slot still names a specific tradition. The Fuzzy slot earns its place by holding both as partly right.

What you get in 30 seconds

Type a question, hit ⌘+Enter. Get a 5-section answer with a confidence score. Click PRESENT for a one-page printable PDF. Click COPY MD for clipboard. Every answer auto-saves to ~/iCloud Drive/Akasha/ as a timestamped markdown file. Optional: toggle FAST (local, private, no internet) versus DEEP (cloud, 70B parameters, free).

The ask

This document set exists to invite scrutiny. The white paper lays out the epistemological argument; the PDR documents the architecture; the stats report exposes the raw benchmark JSON for re-execution. If you find a hole, write to the maintainer. If the framework is sound, the request is two minutes of your attention to validate it on a question of your own.

Try it once. The interface is at http://localhost:3000 while the server runs on the host laptop. Ask: "What did the builders of Göbekli Tepe know that we forgot?" The answer arrives in under 6 seconds, in five layered registers, with a confidence the model is willing to put a number on.
