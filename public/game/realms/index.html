Realm i

Göbekli Tepe

Şanlıurfa, Turkey · c. 9600 BCE

The oldest temple humans built. Twelve T-pillars in a circle. Three carvings to photograph. The makers buried it themselves around 8000 BCE — we do not know why.

Realm ii

Eridu

Southern Mesopotamia · c. 5400 BCE

The first city in the Sumerian King List. The ziggurat of Enki, god of fresh water and craft. Five Atrahasis flood-myth tablets to find — the version that predates Genesis by 1,500 years.

Realm iii

Mohenjo-daro

Indus Valley · c. 2600 BCE

Grid-planned city. No palaces visible. No clear kings. Possibly the most egalitarian ancient civilization ever recorded. The script remains undeciphered.

Realm iv

Teotihuacan

Central Mexico · c. 100 BCE – 550 CE

The place where gods were born. Avenue of the Dead aligned 15.5° east of north. The Aztecs found it abandoned and named it for what it had been.
